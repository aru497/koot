1. The visa money rule, decoded
For a subclass 500 student visa you must prove you can cover one year of tuition + A$29,710 living costs + ~A$2,500 travel. The funds need to be genuine and accessible — savings, an education loan sanction, or a sponsor's funds with evidence. A rough single-student picture:
What you showTypical amount (AUD)
First-year tuition (course-dependent)~$20,000 – $45,000
Living costs (set by Home Affairs)$29,710
Travel / airfare~$2,000 – $2,500
OSHC health cover (whole course)~$500 – $650 / year

An approved education-loan sanction letter is widely accepted as proof of funds — which is why most students sort the loan before lodging the visa.

2. How students actually fund it
Three broad routes. Most families mix a loan with their own savings. Rates, limits and collateral rules change often — treat these as a map, not a quote.

🏦 Public / government banks Lowest rates · secured

Best for larger amounts & lower interest · slower (≈4–6 weeks) · usually need collateral

Government banks lend large sums against collateral (property, FD) at the lowest interest, often with tax benefits under Section 80E. Higher loan ceilings, more paperwork, longer processing.

SBI Global Ed-Vantage ↗ Bank of Baroda ↗ PNB ↗ Canara Bank ↗

⚡ NBFCs Faster · collateral-free options

Best for speed & flexible collateral · ≈2–3 weeks · often need a co-applicant

Non-bank lenders move fast and offer collateral-free loans (typically up to ₹40–75 lakh) with a co-applicant. Interest is higher than public banks, but approval is quicker and university coverage is wide.

Credila (HDFC) ↗ Avanse ↗ Auxilo ↗ InCred ↗

🌍 International lenders No Indian collateral / co-signer

Best if you have no collateral or co-applicant · disbursed in AUD

Cross-border lenders fund select master's programs with no collateral and no co-signer, paid directly in AUD. Eligibility is course- and university-specific. Note: Prodigy Finance supports Australia; MPOWER currently focuses on the US & Canada, not Australia.

4. OSHC — the health cover you can't skip
Overseas Student Health Cover is a condition of your visa. You must buy it before you arrive and keep it for your whole stay — your visa start date and OSHC start date should match. It covers doctor visits, hospital and ambulance, and some medicines, but not dental, optical or physio (you'd add Extras or OVHC for those).

A single-student policy typically runs ~A$500–650 per year, but prices and benefits vary — compare a couple before you buy. Many universities bundle OSHC into your offer; you can usually opt out and arrange your own if it's cheaper.
